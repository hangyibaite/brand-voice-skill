# Analyze

Extract patterns from samples in `assets/` plus live-write responses. Per chosen category, fill out the schema in `references/[category].md` using only evidence from the texts.

## Source weight

Live-write responses outweigh past samples. Past samples are reference. Live-writes are the truth.

## What to extract per category

### Vocabulary
- Words and phrases the user repeats across pieces (the fingerprints)
- Cuss words present, and frequency (per 100 words)
- Filler / transition phrases they reach for
- Phrases that show up multiple times — these are real, not coincidence

### Sentence rhythm
- Average sentence length (words)
- Frequency of fragments (1–3 word sentences) per 100 words
- One-word paragraphs: present or absent
- Punctuation habits: em-dashes, semicolons, ellipses, all caps, parentheses
- Question frequency

### Structural moves
- How they open pieces (first 2 sentences across samples — find the pattern)
- How they close pieces (last 2 sentences across samples)
- Mid-piece patterns: lists, digressions, callbacks, self-interruptions, parenthetical asides
- How they handle examples: concrete with specifics, or abstract

### POV + tone
- First / second / third person dominant
- Relationship to reader: friend, peer, mentor, antagonist
- Emotional register: warm, dry, hostile, playful, deadpan
- Where they swear vs. where they don't

### Formatting
- Headers used or avoided
- Bullets vs. prose ratio
- Bold / italic frequency
- Line break habits

## What not to extract

- Tone adjectives the user volunteered ("casual and funny")
- Generic style labels
- Anything not directly visible in the writing

## Method

1. Read all samples for the category
2. Read the live-write response for the category
3. Underline (mentally) repeats — words, structures, openings
4. Count rough frequencies
5. Pull 2–3 short excerpts that best represent the voice
6. Fill the schema in `references/[category].md`
7. If a schema field has no evidence, ask the user ONE targeted question to fill it

Do not pad. Do not guess. Empty-handed fields get a question.
