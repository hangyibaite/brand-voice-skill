# Intake Protocol

Run this once per chosen category, in this order: long-form, short-form, direct response.

## Hard rules

- Do NOT ask the user to describe their voice
- Do NOT ask "what is your tone"
- Do NOT use style adjectives in questions
- Make them produce raw text or speech you can analyze
- Save every response verbatim. Do not clean it up.

## Step 0: Business context (run ONCE before any category intake)

Ask the user:

> "What is your business about? Name 2–3 things your content should cover — the topics you come back to again and again."

Wait for their answer. Save it. This is your topic brief. Use it to generate specific prompts for every category below. Do not use generic fallback topics if they answered this.

## Long-form intake

Use their business context to generate ONE specific topic they should write about. Make it concrete — name the exact angle, not a category.

Say to the user:

> "Write 200–400 words on [specific topic drawn from their business context]. Type it or use voice dictation. Do not edit. If you stumble or ramble, leave it in."

Wait. Save raw.

## Short-form intake

Use their business context to generate ONE specific topic for a short video. Make it something their audience would actually watch — a pain point, a hot take, or a fast how-to from their world.

Say to the user:

> "You're filming a 45-second video about [specific topic drawn from their business context]. Speak it using voice dictation exactly how you'd say it on camera. Or type it as a script. Include stage directions if you use them. Do not edit."

Wait. Save raw.

## Direct response intake

Use their business context to generate ONE specific piece — name the product, the audience, or the situation concretely.

Say to the user:

> "Write or dictate this exactly how you'd send it: [specific direct response piece drawn from their business context — e.g. 'an email to coaches on your list pitching your landing page course' not 'an email about a workshop']."

Wait. Save raw.

## After all chosen intakes are done

Confirm to the user:

> "Got it. Moving to analysis."

Proceed to Step 4 of the main SKILL.md.
