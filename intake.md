# Intake Protocol

Run this once per chosen category, in this order: long-form, short-form, direct response.

## Hard rules

- Do NOT ask the user to describe their voice
- Do NOT ask "what is your tone"
- Do NOT use style adjectives in questions
- Make them produce raw text or speech you can analyze
- Save every response verbatim. Do not clean it up.
- You MUST ask at least 5 questions before generating anything. Do not skip ahead.
- You MUST collect at least 5 attachment references of existing content (posts, comments, threads, tweets, writing, reels, captions, emails, etc.) before proceeding to analysis. Ask for up to 10. Do not accept fewer than 5.

## Step 0: Business context (run ONCE before any category intake)

Ask the user:

> "What is your business about? Name 2–3 things your content should cover — the topics you come back to again and again."

Wait for their answer. Save it. This is your topic brief. Use it to generate specific prompts for every category below. Do not use generic fallback topics if they answered this.

## Step 0.5: ICP — Ideal Client Profile

Ask the user:

> "Describe your ICP — your audience and exact ideal client profile. Who are you talking to? Be specific: their role, pain points, what stage they're at, what they want. If you already have a reference document for your ICP, paste it in here."

Wait for their answer. Save it. This shapes every prompt you generate — the language, the angle, the examples should all speak to this person.

## Step 0.7: Collect existing content references

Say to the user:

> "I need to study how you actually write. Paste or attach at least 5 (ideally 10) pieces of your existing content — posts, comments, threads, tweets, reels scripts, captions, emails, newsletter issues, sales pages, community replies, anything you wrote and published. The more variety, the better the voice capture. Drop them here or into the assets folder."

Wait. Do not proceed until you have at least 5 pieces. If the user provides fewer than 5, ask again:

> "I need at least 5 real pieces to build an accurate voice profile. You've given me [X] so far. Can you add [5 - X] more?"

Save everything verbatim.

## Long-form intake

Use their business context and ICP to generate ONE specific topic they should write about. Make it concrete — name the exact angle, not a category.

Say to the user:

> "Write 200–400 words on [specific topic drawn from their business context]. Type it or use voice dictation. Do not edit. If you stumble or ramble, leave it in."

Then immediately add:

> "If you want a more accurate topic, describe what your content is usually about in more detail and I'll give you more options."

If the user provides more detail, generate 3–5 alternative topic options based on their description. Let them pick one. Then use their selection as the prompt and ask them to write on that topic instead.

Wait. Save raw.

## Short-form intake

Use their business context and ICP to generate ONE specific topic for a short video. Make it something their audience would actually watch — a pain point, a hot take, or a fast how-to from their world.

Say to the user:

> "You're filming a 45-second video about [specific topic drawn from their business context]. Speak it using voice dictation exactly how you'd say it on camera. Or type it as a script. Include stage directions if you use them. Do not edit."

Then immediately add:

> "If you want a more accurate topic, describe what your content is usually about in more detail and I'll give you more options."

If the user provides more detail, generate 3–5 alternative topic options based on their description. Let them pick one. Then use their selection as the prompt and ask them to write on that topic instead.

Wait. Save raw.

## Direct response intake

Use their business context and ICP to generate ONE specific piece — name the product, the audience, or the situation concretely.

Say to the user:

> "Write or dictate this exactly how you'd send it: [specific direct response piece drawn from their business context — e.g. 'an email to coaches on your list pitching your landing page course' not 'an email about a workshop']."

Then immediately add:

> "If you want a more accurate topic, describe what your content is usually about in more detail and I'll give you more options."

If the user provides more detail, generate 3–5 alternative topic options based on their description. Let them pick one. Then use their selection as the prompt and ask them to write on that topic instead.

Wait. Save raw.

## After all chosen intakes are done

Count the questions you asked. If fewer than 5, ask additional targeted questions about their content habits, audience engagement patterns, or content creation process until you hit at least 5.

Confirm to the user:

> "Got it. Moving to analysis."

Proceed to Step 4 of the main SKILL.md.
